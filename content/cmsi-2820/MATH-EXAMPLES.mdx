# LaTeX Math in MDX

This guide shows you how to use mathematical notation in your lecture notes using LaTeX syntax with MathJax.

> **üí° Tip**: Right-click any math expression to access MathJax's context menu! You can copy the LaTeX source, zoom in/out, adjust settings, and more.

## Inline Math

Use single dollar signs `$...$` for inline math:

The quadratic formula is $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$ and it's useful for solving equations.

For sets, we can write $A = \{1, 2, 3\}$ and $B = \{x \mid x > 0\}$.

The sum formula is $\sum_{i=1}^{n} i = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$ which is a classic result.

## Display Math (Block)

Use double dollar signs `$$...$$` for centered display math:

$$
\sqrt{a^2 + b^2}
$$

### Complex Equations

$$
E = mc^2
$$

$$
\int_{a}^{b} f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)
$$

$$
\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1}{x} = 0
$$

## Discrete Math Examples

### Set Theory

**Inline**: The cardinality of set $A$ is denoted $|A|$ or $\#A$.

**Display**:

$$
A \cup B = \{x \mid x \in A \lor x \in B\}
$$

$$
A \cap B = \{x \mid x \in A \land x \in B\}
$$

$$
A \setminus B = \{x \mid x \in A \land x \notin B\}
$$

**Power Set**:

$$
\mathcal{P}(A) = \{S \mid S \subseteq A\}
$$

### Logic

**Logical Operators**:

- Conjunction: $P \land Q$
- Disjunction: $P \lor Q$
- Negation: $\neg P$
- Implication: $P \to Q$
- Biconditional: $P \leftrightarrow Q$

**Quantifiers**:

$$
\forall x \in \mathbb{N}, \, x \geq 0
$$

$$
\exists x \in \mathbb{R}, \, x^2 = 2
$$

### Number Theory

**Divisibility**:

$$
a \mid b \iff \exists k \in \mathbb{Z}, \, b = ak
$$

**Modular Arithmetic**:

$$
a \equiv b \pmod{n} \iff n \mid (a - b)
$$

**GCD and LCM**:

$$
\gcd(a, b) \cdot \text{lcm}(a, b) = |ab|
$$

### Combinatorics

**Permutations**:

$$
P(n, r) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}
$$

**Combinations**:

$$
\binom{n}{r} = C(n, r) = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}
$$

**Binomial Theorem**:

$$
(x + y)^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} x^{n-k} y^k
$$

### Sequences and Series

**Arithmetic Sequence**:

$$
a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d
$$

**Geometric Sequence**:

$$
a_n = a_1 \cdot r^{n-1}
$$

**Sum of Arithmetic Series**:

$$
\sum_{i=1}^{n} i = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}
$$

**Sum of Geometric Series**:

$$
\sum_{i=0}^{n} r^i = \frac{1 - r^{n+1}}{1 - r}, \quad r \neq 1
$$

### Functions

**Function Definition**:

$$
f: A \to B
$$

**Composition**:

$$
(g \circ f)(x) = g(f(x))
$$

**Inverse**:

$$
f^{-1}(f(x)) = x \text{ and } f(f^{-1}(y)) = y
$$

### Graph Theory

**Degree Sum Formula**:

$$
\sum_{v \in V} \deg(v) = 2|E|
$$

**Handshaking Lemma**:

$$
\text{Number of vertices with odd degree is even}
$$

### Probability

**Basic Probability**:

$$
P(A) = \frac{|A|}{|S|}
$$

**Conditional Probability**:

$$
P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}
$$

**Bayes' Theorem**:

$$
P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(B \mid A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)}
$$

## Mathematical Induction

**Base Case**: Prove $P(1)$ is true.

**Inductive Step**: Assume $P(k)$ is true for some $k \geq 1$, then prove $P(k+1)$ is true.

**Example Proof**:

$$
\text{Prove: } \sum_{i=1}^{n} i = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}
$$

**Base Case** ($n = 1$):

$$
\sum_{i=1}^{1} i = 1 = \frac{1(1+1)}{2} = \frac{2}{2} = 1 \quad \checkmark
$$

**Inductive Step**: Assume true for $n = k$:

$$
\sum_{i=1}^{k} i = \frac{k(k+1)}{2}
$$

Prove for $n = k + 1$:

$$
\begin{align*}
\sum_{i=1}^{k+1} i &= \left(\sum_{i=1}^{k} i\right) + (k+1) \\
&= \frac{k(k+1)}{2} + (k+1) \\
&= \frac{k(k+1) + 2(k+1)}{2} \\
&= \frac{(k+1)(k+2)}{2} \quad \checkmark
\end{align*}
$$

## Tips for Writing Math in MDX

### 1. Escape Underscores in Text

If you write about `file_name` in regular text, underscores work fine. But in math mode, underscores are subscripts: $x_1, x_2, x_3$.

### 2. Use `\text{}` for Words in Math

$$
P(x) = \text{true if } x > 0
$$

### 3. Spacing in Math

- Thin space: `\,` as in $a\,b$
- Medium space: `\:` as in $a\:b$
- Large space: `\;` as in $a\;b$
- Quad space: `\quad` as in $a\quad b$

### 4. Common Mistakes

‚ùå **Wrong**: `$A = {1, 2, 3}$` (still need to escape braces!)  
‚úÖ **Correct**: `$A = \{1, 2, 3\}$`

‚ùå **Wrong**: `$$x = 5 $$` (spaces inside delimiters)  
‚úÖ **Correct**: `$$x = 5$$` (no spaces)

$$
